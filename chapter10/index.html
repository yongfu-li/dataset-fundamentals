Chapter 10 — Synthetic Data Generation

Follow the path in order: read the bridge, watch the part, then take the quiz before moving on. Downloads are available if you prefer to study offline.

What you will learn

  • Define synthetic data and explain its benefits for privacy, scarcity, and augmentation
  • Anticipate validation gaps, bias amplification, and diversity limits in synthetic datasets
  • Apply statistical methods including distribution fitting, Monte Carlo, bootstrapping, and regression
  • Explain GAN architecture, training dynamics, variants, evaluation metrics, and deepfake risks
  • Describe LLM-based synthesis for text, labels, and structured records with audit gates
  • Recognize synthetic-data use cases in healthcare, finance, autonomy, and cybersecurity
  • Evaluate ethical risks including privacy claims, over-reliance, quality, and regulatory compliance
  • Follow best practices for transparency, validation, and oversight before deployment

Learning path

Before part 1 Chapters 2 through 9 covered how real records are collected, labeled, cleaned, documented, and scaled. This chapter asks when artificially generated data can supplement or replace scarce or sensitive observations. This opening part defines synthetic data, cross-modal fidelity, and benefits for privacy, rare scenarios, and model training.
