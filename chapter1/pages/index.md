# Chapter 1 — Introduction to Datasets

This chapter builds a shared vocabulary for datasets: what they are, how they are typed and stored, how to judge quality, how to explore a new file, where organizations put datasets to work, and why management and privacy protect that investment.

**Full deck:** [slides.md](../slides.md) · [chapter1.pptx](../chapter1.pptx)

## Objectives

- Define a dataset as curated records and attributes prepared for a purpose
- Classify datasets by structure, temporal behavior, and storage format
- Assess quality along accuracy, completeness, consistency, relevance, and timeliness
- Apply a first-pass exploration mindset before modeling
- Recognize how organizations put datasets to work and why management matters

## Learning path

This chapter starts from the most basic question: what counts as a dataset? This first clip defines records and attributes, then shows how the same idea appears in CSV tables and JSON documents—the vocabulary every later clip builds on.
