## Learning objective

Recognize petabyte-scale volume as the trigger for distributed systems beyond conventional DBMS.

## Chapter context

Section 9.4.1 characterizes big data dimensions and why conventional DBMS fail at petabyte scale. Data generated by social media platforms, sensors, and internet transactions can easily reach petabytes (1 petabyte = 1 million gigabytes) or more.

## What this example shows

Data generated by social media platforms, sensors, and internet transactions can easily reach petabytes (1 petabyte = 1 million gigabytes) or more.

## Key terms

- **Volume** — Sheer size of data beyond conventional DBMS capacity.
- **Veracity** — Quality and reliability of noisy or incomplete big-data streams.
