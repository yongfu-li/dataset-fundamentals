Transcript

Slide 1 — Chapter 11 — Active learning applications

Query strategies matter only when they change real labeling queues. This part walks active-learning applications in natural language processing, driving perception, and medical imaging, showing how uncertain items reach human experts while easy cases stay unlabeled.

Slide 2 — Learning objectives

By the end of this part, learners should be able to describe active learning for review sentiment and related NLP tasks; explain uncertainty-driven queues for driving perception; and interpret medical examples for pneumonia X-rays and dermoscopic lesions.

Slide 3 — Active learning in NLP

In NLP, active learning reduces labeled volume for classification, named entity recognition, sentiment analysis, and related tasks. Annotating large corpora is expensive, especially for legal or medical text. The model focuses labeling on informative or uncertain samples such as ironic, mixed, or domain-specific phrases.

Slide 4 — Example 11.9 — Active Learning for Review Sentiment

Example 11.9 queues uncertain reviews for human sentiment labels. A sentiment model trained on a small seed set uses uncertainty or entropy sampling to request labels on ironic or mixed reviews, improving nuance with fewer total annotations. The example 9 module for this chapter summarizes that sentiment queue.

Slide 5 — Active learning in vision

In computer vision, labeling images or videos for detection, segmentation, or action recognition is time-consuming. Active learning focuses annotators on frames that most improve performance, such as occlusions, weather degradation, or ambiguous poses, instead of labeling every fleet frame or every video frame.

Slide 6 — Example 11.10 — Active Learning for Driving Perception

Example 11.10 focuses annotators on occluded or foggy driving scenes. Uncertainty sampling queries frames where pedestrians or signs are occluded or weather-degraded, so perception models learn rare hard cases without labeling every fleet frame. The example 10 module for this chapter describes that perception queue.

Slide 7 — Healthcare case study

Medical image labeling requires domain expertise and is costly. Active learning reduces the number of expert annotations while routing the most informative studies to clinicians. Experts spend limited time on atypical or overlapping pathologies rather than routine easy cases.

Slide 8 — Example 11.12 — Pneumonia Detection with Uncertain X-rays

Example 11.12 sends atypical chest X-rays to radiologists via active learning. Uncertainty sampling surfaces X-rays with atypical pneumonia or overlapping pathologies for radiologist review, improving edge-case detection with fewer labeled studies. The example 12 module for this chapter frames that pneumonia queue.

Slide 9 — Example 11.13 — Dermoscopic Lesion Uncertainty Queue

Example 11.13 prioritizes ambiguous skin lesions for dermatologist labels. Active learning queues atypical or rare dermoscopic images for expert labeling so the classifier improves on early melanoma and other hard lesions without labeling every image. The example 13 module for this chapter covers that dermatology queue.

Slide 10 — Takeaways

Across NLP, driving perception, and medical imaging, active learning concentrates human effort on uncertain, atypical, or safety-critical cases. Seed models plus selective expert review cut annotation volume while improving edge-case performance. Routine easy items need not consume expert hours.
