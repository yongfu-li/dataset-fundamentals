What you will learn

  • Contrast active learning, weak supervision, self-supervised learning, and hybrid crowd–expert pipelines
  • Explain the active-learning loop and common query strategies such as uncertainty, margin, and entropy sampling
  • Apply diversity, cost-sensitive, and model-in-the-loop techniques when selecting items to label
  • Recognize active-learning use cases in NLP, driving perception, and medical imaging
  • Define weak supervision and programmatic labeling with labeling functions and Snorkel-style aggregation
  • Describe self-supervised pretext tasks and contrastive pretraining before fine-tuning
  • Compare crowdsourcing and expert annotation on cost, quality, and domain risk
  • Design hybrid pipelines that combine crowd scale with expert gold sets and hard-case routing

Learning path

Before part 1 Chapter 4 covered annotation modalities, workflows, tools, and quality control. This chapter does not repeat that foundation. It develops four strategies that reduce expert labeling cost at scale: active learning, weak supervision, self-supervised representation learning, and hybrid crowd–expert pipelines.
