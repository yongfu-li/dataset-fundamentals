Chapter 3 — Ethics and Privacy in Data Work

Follow the path in order: read the bridge, watch the part, then take the quiz before moving on. Downloads are available if you prefer to study offline.

Full chapter PPTXFull chapter PDFStart first part →Browse examples

What you will learn

  • Distinguish ethics from privacy and map stakeholders across the dataset lifecycle
  • Apply core ethical principles as operational design tests, not post-hoc slogans
  • Implement consent, minimization, and Privacy by Design as privacy-management practice
  • Compare anonymization, encryption, differential privacy, and federated learning
  • Recognize selection and algorithmic bias and connect ethics overview to Chapter 7 depth
  • Navigate GDPR, HIPAA, and CCPA as planning maps for data teams
  • Analyze breach failure modes, consequences, and prevention controls
  • Use utilitarian, rights-based, virtue, and justice lenses for ethical decisions
  • Anticipate AI-scale surveillance, fragmented regulation, and emerging privacy tools

Learning path
