Chapter 9 — Advanced Data Collection Techniques

Follow the path in order: read the bridge, watch the part, then take the quiz before moving on. Downloads are available if you prefer to study offline.

Full chapter PPTXFull chapter PDFStart first part →Browse examples

What you will learn

  • Explain when traditional collection pipelines hit scale, speed, and heterogeneity limits
  • Design crowdsourcing campaigns with clear tasks, quality controls, and ethical labor practices
  • Compare MTurk and alternative crowd platforms for microtasks and research recruitment
  • Describe IoT sensing components and edge computing benefits for continuous collection
  • Anticipate IoT security, privacy, scalability, and data-overload challenges
  • Characterize big data volume, velocity, variety, and veracity for collection design
  • Distinguish streaming ingestion, data lakes, and data warehouses for operational storage
  • Connect advanced collection patterns to real-time sentiment, distributed storage, and AI pipelines

Learning path

Before part 1 Chapter 2 covered surveys, sampling, and small-scale collection. This chapter asks what changes when evidence must arrive continuously, from many devices, or at volumes no spreadsheet can hold. This opening part motivates advanced techniques—crowdsourcing, IoT sensing, and streaming pipelines—without abandoning decision-focused collection design.
