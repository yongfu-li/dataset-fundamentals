## Slide 3 — What the Internet of Things is

The Internet of Things is a network of physical devices that connect to the internet and exchange data. Devices range from smart thermostats and wearables to industrial machinery, autonomous vehicles, and smart-city systems. Objects collect, share, and analyze data with limited direct human intervention, yielding timely status and environmental insights.

## Slide 4 — Core components: devices, connectivity, processing

At its core, IoT combines things equipped with sensors or actuators; reliable connectivity to move data; and processing analytics at the edge or in the cloud. Devices capture measurements or perform actions. Networks carry those readings onward. Processing turns raw streams into decisions for users and automated systems.

## Slide 7 — Connectivity options for IoT transfer

Devices need reliable links to gateways or cloud services. Common options include Wi-Fi for home and office devices; Bluetooth Low Energy for many wearables; cellular networks for long-range mobile devices; and low-power wide-area networks for small payloads over long distances, as in agriculture. Choice depends on power, range, and bandwidth.
