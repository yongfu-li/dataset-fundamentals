Chapter 7 — Dataset Bias and Fairness

Follow the path in order: read the bridge, watch the part, then take the quiz before moving on. Downloads are available if you prefer to study offline.

Start first part →Browse examples

What you will learn

  • Define dataset bias and fairness and explain why they affect trust and accountability
  • Distinguish sampling, measurement, historical, label, aggregation, and confirmation bias
  • Use distribution checks, correlations, disparate impact, and visualizations to detect bias
  • Explain ethical, social, legal, and business impacts of biased datasets
  • Compare procedural fairness, outcome fairness, equalized odds, calibration, and accuracy trade-offs
  • Select pre-processing, in-processing, and post-processing mitigation strategies
  • Apply best practices for fair data collection, labeling, validation, and auditing
  • Recognize how AIF360 and Fairlearn support fairness measurement and mitigation
  • Anticipate emerging fairness challenges in generative AI, federated learning, and regulation
