Chapter 13 — Reproducibility and Open Science

Follow the path in order: read the bridge, watch the part, then take the quiz before moving on. Downloads are available if you prefer to study offline.

Full chapter PPTXFull chapter PDFStart first part →Browse examples

What you will learn

  • Distinguish reproducibility from replicability and state core open-science principles
  • Anticipate privacy, documentation, and standards barriers that block reproducible work
  • Apply documentation, metadata, and pinned dataset versions for research-facing reuse
  • Explain FAIR principles and data-quality practices that support reproducibility
  • Describe provenance, licensing, privacy, and ethical constraints on open data sharing
  • Integrate notebooks, containers, and workflow files into reproducible research pipelines
  • Use continuous integration and automated documentation to keep analyses checkable
  • Employ R Markdown, DVC, collaboration platforms, and audit trails for team-scale reproducibility
